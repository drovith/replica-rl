Origin / 2019

A print shop
and a grudge

REFUSE began in a damp ex-commercial print unit off Oldham Road, Manchester — two screens, a flash dryer, and a box of blank heavyweight cotton bought with the last of a student overdraft.

We were tired of paying £90 for a t-shirt that was a logo and nothing else; tired of “limited editions” that quietly reappeared the moment they sold; tired of brands with marketing departments bigger than their print rooms.

So we made the opposite. Small runs. Loud graphics. Honest construction. Everything pulled by hand on the same two screens we started with — now joined by a few more, but the ethos hasn’t moved an inch.

Six things we refuse

The rules

Pinned above the dryer since day one. We break a lot of layout grids. We don’t break these.

01

No restocks

When a piece sells out, it’s finished. We will not reprint it, recolour it, or sneak it back next season. Scarcity isn’t a tactic here — it’s the whole point. You buy a moment, not an open tab.

02

No trend-chasing

We don’t read the forecasts. Each collection starts from a subculture, a record, a city, a feeling — never from a trend report telling us what teal will do in spring. If it dates badly, good. It meant something at the time.

03

No rented logos

Every graphic on a REFUSE garment is drawn in-house. No licensed characters, no clip-art borrowed swagger. If you see a mark on our cotton, someone in the studio bled over it.

04

No hidden weight

We list the GSM, the fabric source, and the print method on every product, because the spec is the product. A 500gsm hoodie should feel like one. We’d rather lose a sale to honesty than win one with a marketing word.

05

No throwaway pricing

We don’t race the bottom and we don’t inflate to discount. The price is the real cost of small-batch, fairly-paid, heavyweight production — printed down the road, not across an ocean. No fake sales. Ever.

06

No blending in

The brief for every drop is the same: would you cross a room to read it? If a piece is polite, it doesn’t ship. We make clothes that argue with their surroundings — confrontational by design, never by accident.

From screen to seal

How it’s made

Step 01

Cut small

Blanks are cut in batches of a few hundred from organic and recycled cotton, milled in Portugal and sewn by a family workshop we’ve used since 2020.

Step 02

Print by hand

Every graphic is pulled on our own carousel using water-based and discharge inks — softer hand, no plastic crackle, lower waste. Misprints become studio one-offs, not landfill.

Step 03

Number & seal

Each piece gets a hand-stamped run number on the inner tag. Once the run ships, the screens are reclaimed and the artwork is retired to the archive for good.
