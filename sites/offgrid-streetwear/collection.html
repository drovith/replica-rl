Concrete
Gospel

Faith for a city that pours itself in concrete. Fourteen pieces about devotion to the unglamorous: tower blocks, night buses, the religion of a Friday. Live now.

Live now 14 pieces Hand-numbered No restock
Collection note

We shot Concrete Gospel at 5am across three estates we grew up near. The palette is poured concrete, sodium streetlight and the off-white of a worn-in tee — broken only by the acid flash of a fire door.

Graphics borrow from devotional printing: chapter headers, stamped psalms, the cheavy serif of a hymn book, redrawn with a craft knife. The clothes are built to be lived hard in — and to look better the more you wreck them.
