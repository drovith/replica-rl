Margaret Halloran, Founding Partner

“The contract records the deal. My job is to make sure the deal is one my client can live with five years from now.”

Margaret has spent more than thirty years at the negotiating table, the last fifteen as a founding partner of Halloran Voss. Before co-founding the firm she chaired the mid-market M&A group of a global practice, where she grew frustrated that the clients who most needed seasoned judgment were too often handed the least experienced lawyers.

She is known for a calm, structural approach to negotiation — reframing a stalemate as a problem of sequencing or risk allocation rather than a contest of wills. Clients return to her for the combinations that matter most: founder exits, board-level divestitures, and the combinations where reputation and relationship must survive the deal.

Margaret writes and speaks regularly on earnout structures and special-committee process, and she mentors the firm's younger lawyers in the craft of getting to yes without giving away the store.
