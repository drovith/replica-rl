Visit & booking

Holding a place is the easy part.

No accounts, no rush, no pressure. Tell us which program calls to you and we'll hold your room for seven days, with nothing to pay, while you arrange the time off and the journey down. The hard part — actually allowing yourself to go — we'll help with too.

How a booking works

Four unhurried steps.

01

Choose your dates

Pick a program and a set of dates from the programs page. If you're unsure which suits you, write and we'll talk it through honestly.

02

We hold your room

Email or call and we'll hold a place for seven days, free, while you sort the practicalities. Tell us your room preference and any access needs then.

03

Confirm with a deposit

A £150 deposit secures the booking; the balance is due four weeks before you arrive. We'll send a short note about what to bring — which is very little.

04

Arrive and stop

Come down the lane any time after 3pm on the first day. We'll take your bag, pour you tea, and the rest of the world can wait at the gate.

Getting here

Far enough to feel away, near enough to reach.

Fallow sits at the end of a single-track lane near Hay-on-Wye, on the Welsh side of the border, where the Marches fold up into the Black Mountains. The last mile is unpaved and beautiful; drive it slowly.

  • By car3 hrs from London · 1 hr from Hereford
  • By trainHereford station, then our pickup
  • PickupFree from Hereford on arrival day
  • Nearest townHay-on-Wye, 15 minutes

We run a free minibus from Hereford station at 2.30pm on the first day and back for the late-morning trains on the last. Just tell us your train when you book.

Before you ask

Questions, answered plainly.

I've never done yoga. Will I be lost?

Not at all — a good half of our guests are beginners or returning after years away. Every practice is gentle and offered with options. There is nothing to keep up with and no one watching. If you can lie on a floor and breathe, you can do everything here.

Is the silence compulsory?

Never. Held silence is offered — a single morning on the weekends, a day or two on the longer programs — and most people come to treasure it. But you are always free to opt out, speak to a teacher, or simply walk during those hours.

Can you cater for my diet?

Yes, and gladly. Our kitchen is largely plant-based already and we handle allergies, intolerances and most ways of eating without drama. Tell us when you book and you'll never have to raise it at the table.

Do I need to bring anything?

Very little. Comfortable layers, sturdy shoes for the lanes, and something warm — Welsh evenings are cool year-round. Mats, props, blankets, towels, boots and waterproofs are all here. Leave the laptop at home; it won't have much to do.

Is there phone signal and wifi?

Signal is patchy by nature, not by rule — the valley simply doesn't carry it well. There is wifi in the office for anything genuinely urgent, but most guests find that being a little unreachable is the whole point.

What's your cancellation policy?

Deposits are refundable up to four weeks before arrival, after which we ask for the full balance, as we will likely have turned others away. We're human about genuine emergencies, and we strongly recommend simple travel insurance.

Can I come on my own?

Most people do. Solo guests are the heart of every group, and the small numbers and shared table mean no one stays a stranger for long. Come alone with complete confidence.
