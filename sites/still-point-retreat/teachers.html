The teachers

A small faculty who teach less, on purpose.

We keep our teaching circle deliberately tiny. You'll practise with the same two or three people across your whole stay, long enough that they learn how you move and what you flinch from. No rotating timetable of strangers, no celebrity guests — just steady, attentive company.

Maren

Founder & lead teacher

Maren Llewellyn

Hatha & Restorative · teaching since 2003

Maren trained in Mysore and Devon and spent fifteen years on the international retreat circuit before it nearly broke her. She came home to Wales, bought a failing farm, and built the kind of place she'd needed all along. Her morning practice is slow, precise and quietly funny, with a deep suspicion of the word "more."

"I'm not here to make you bendier," she says. "I'm here to help your body believe it's safe enough to stop holding on." She leads the dawn hatha and the held silences, and you'll usually find her in the garden by mid-afternoon.

The rest of the circle

The people you'll share the days with.

T

Tomas Eira

Breath & Stillness

A former emergency physician who left medicine for the breath after a decade of night shifts. Tomas holds the silent sittings and the long, slow pranayama. He has a steadying, low-voiced calm that the whole room seems to borrow.

A

Ana Okonkwo

Yin & Somatics

Ana teaches the dusk yin and the floor-based somatic work, with a rare gift for making a room of strangers feel instantly unhurried. She trained in body-based trauma practice and is endlessly patient with stiff hips and tired backs.

D

Dafydd Pryce

Walking & the Land

Born three valleys over, Dafydd knows every lane, badger sett and old drovers' path on this land. He leads the morning walks and the optional silent rambles, and can name most of what's flowering before you've noticed it.

N

Nia Vaughan

Kitchen & Nourishment

Nia cooks the way her grandmother did — seasonal, vegetable-forward, generous. She runs the kitchen and the occasional bread morning, and her food is, by quiet consensus, half the reason people return.

R

Ruth Adeyemi

Restorative & Sleep

Ruth specialises in deep rest and the science of sleep, leading the evening restorative sessions and offering one-to-one work on the longer programs. She is, several guests have noted, impossible to stay tense around.

G

Gwen Harte

Sound & Stillness

Gwen brings the occasional evening sound practice — singing bowls, the harmonium, and long resonant quiet by the fire. A musician first, she found her way to this work through grief and stayed for the stillness.

How we teach

No one here will adjust you into a shape you don't want to be in. We teach to the body in front of us, on the day it arrives — and most days, the deepest work is simply learning to do less, more gently.
