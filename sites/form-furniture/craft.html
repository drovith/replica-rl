Craft & materials

Made
to mend.

Good furniture is mostly decisions you can't see: which oak, which tannage, which joint, which screw. We make those decisions in one workshop, with one short list of materials, and we keep every part in stock so a piece can be repaired instead of replaced.

Five materials

A short list, deeply known

Oiled European oak

Slow-grown, quarter-sawn for stability, and finished with a plant-based hard-wax oil rather than lacquer. It can be re-oiled and sanded for a lifetime; lacquer can't. Smoked walnut is offered as an alternate.

Blackened mild steel

Hot-blackened, not painted, so the finish is in the metal and won't chip to a bright edge. We use it where strength has to stay slim — table aprons, lamp stems, the hardware you can see.

Vegetable-tanned leather

Tanned with bark, not chrome, from a single Tuscan tannery. It starts firm and pale, then darkens and softens with use. We grade for substance over uniformity — small marks are kept, not buffed away.

Undyed Italian wool

A heavy, tight basket-weave in the natural colours of the fleece — bone, stone, charcoal. No dye means no fade, and the lanolin still in the fibre helps it shrug off spills.

Honed travertine

Quarried near Tivoli and honed to a matte, open surface that catches light softly. Every top is unique in its veining; we cut adjoining pieces from the same block so a pair always relates.

In the workshop

From board to piece

Four steps, all under one roof in Brianza, the cabinet-making region north of Milan. No piece leaves until it has been sat in, levelled, and signed.

Step 01

Select

Boards are chosen by hand and rested in the workshop until their moisture matches the room they'll live in.

Step 02

Joint

Frames are cut with through-tenons and dowels. We avoid hidden glue blocks so joints can be opened and re-set.

Step 03

Finish

Surfaces are hand-sanded through the grits and oiled by hand — never sprayed — so the finish can be renewed at home.

Step 04

Sign

Each piece is numbered, stamped with its maker's mark, and logged so we can trace its parts for the next decade.

The guarantee

Ten years, and a plan for after

Every PLINTH piece carries a ten-year structural guarantee. More usefully, we keep every component — cushions, slings, glides, hardware, glass — in stock for the life of the design, and we publish the drawings so any good maker can help if we're far away.

Send us a tired cushion and we'll refill it. Send a photo of a loose joint and we'll send the key and the steps. The aim is simple: the piece you buy should be the last one you need.
