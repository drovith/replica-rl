Farms & suppliers

The growers who really make the menu.

We're only as good as the people who supply us — twenty-three farms, smallholdings, fishers and foragers working the land and water around Harlow Creek. Here are a few of them.

Our supply, by the numbers

Short journeys, fair prices

Buying this way is slower and more expensive than calling a national wholesaler. It also means our ingredients are picked at their peak, our money stays in the valley, and we can stand behind every word on the menu. We think that's a fair trade.

Meet the suppliers

Supplier 01

Maggie Doran · Wren Field Market Garden

2 miles · vegetables & salad leaves

Maggie grows on three acres where the Wren meets the Harlow, working without chemicals and with a near-obsessive attention to flavour over yield. Her polytunnels keep us in tomatoes deep into November and her winter leaves carry our salads through the cold months.

  • Heritage tomatoes, ridge cucumbers, summer leaves
  • Crown prince & delica squash through autumn
  • Winter chicories, kale and rainbow chard
Supplier 02

The Aldous Brothers · Tamworth pork

11 miles · rare-breed pork & duck eggs

Sam and Joe Aldous raise Tamworth pigs the slow way — outdoors, on pasture and orchard windfalls, taking twice as long to grow as an intensive herd. The difference is in the fat, which carries flavour the way a good apple carries scent. Their ducks supply our eggs.

  • Pasture-raised, slow-grown Tamworth pork
  • Free-range duck eggs, year round
  • Bones and trotters for the stock pot
Supplier 03

Niamh Brennan · Hedgerow forager

Roaming · wild fruit, herbs & mushrooms

Niamh walks the lanes, woods and riverbanks nobody else bothers with, and arrives at our back door with whatever the wild is offering: damsons and sloes in autumn, wild garlic in spring, chanterelles after rain. Everything she brings is logged, seasonal and gathered responsibly.

  • Hedgerow damsons, sloes, elderflower & rosehips
  • Wild garlic, sorrel, alexanders in spring
  • Foraged mushrooms in season
Supplier 04

Stell Field Farm · Dexter beef & lamb

7 miles · pasture-raised beef & lamb

The Okafor family graze native Dexter cattle and a small flock of sheep on herb-rich meadows above the village. Their animals are reared entirely on grass, slaughtered locally, and hung properly — which is why our beef pie tastes the way beef used to.

  • Grass-fed, dry-aged Dexter beef
  • Meadow-raised hogget and lamb
  • Beets, walnuts and orchard fruit from the farm shop
Supplier 05

Tilbrook Watermill · stoneground flour

5 miles · heritage grain & flour

One working watermill talking to another. Tilbrook mills heritage wheat — Maris Widgeon, spelt, emmer — between French burr stones turned by the same river that once powered our building. Their flour is the backbone of our daily sourdough and every sheet of pasta we roll.

  • Stoneground bread & pasta flours
  • Heritage and ancient grain varieties
  • Pinhead oats for our crumbles and porridge
Supplier 06

Foxdenton Orchard · cider & apples

2 miles · cider, apples & cobnuts

A traditional orchard of standard trees, some over a century old, pressing unhurried cider from named apple varieties most supermarkets have forgotten. Their dry still cider sharpens our pork sauce, and their russets and cobnuts turn up across the autumn menu.

  • Dry still and lightly sparkling ciders
  • Russets, bramleys and rare dessert apples
  • Kentish cobnuts and orchard honey
