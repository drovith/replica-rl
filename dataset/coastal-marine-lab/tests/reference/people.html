People

A small, interdisciplinary group of faculty, researchers, students, and field staff — most of whom you will find on the water at some point each week.

Faculty & principal investigators

EM

Dr. Elena Marsh

Director · Coastal climate adaptation

A coastal ecologist and the lab's director since 2016. Works at the seam between field ecology and community planning, leading the working-waterfront scenario program.

emarsh@calderwood.edu · Publications

DO

Dr. Daniel Okafor

Professor · Fisheries science

Leads the shifting-stocks program and the four-decade winter trawl survey. Specializes in population genetics and the redistribution of fished species under warming.

dokafor@calderwood.edu · Publications

PV

Dr. Priya Venkataraman

Professor · Estuarine ecology

Studies salt marshes, eelgrass, and mudflats and how the living margin migrates with sea level. Maintains the lab's longest-running rocky-shore survey.

pvenkat@calderwood.edu · Publications

SR

Dr. Sofia Reyes

Associate Professor · Biogeochemistry

Tracks carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen through the bay and its sediments. Her analysis underpins the state's seasonal hypoxia advisory for upper Penobscot Bay.

sreyes@calderwood.edu · Publications

JH

Dr. James Holloway

Research Professor · Physical oceanography

Runs the bay circulation model that interprets Sentinel observations and forecasts stratification, flushing, and the spread of warm-water plankton.

jholloway@calderwood.edu · Publications

LC

Dr. Lydia Chen

Assistant Professor · Coastal social science

Joint with the planning school. Documents local and Indigenous coastal knowledge and studies how communities make decisions under environmental uncertainty.

lchen@calderwood.edu · Publications

Postdoctoral & research scientists

NA

Dr. Noor Abadi

Postdoctoral Researcher

Models landward marsh migration against parcels and roads to find where habitat can — and cannot — retreat as the sea rises.

TF

Dr. Thomas Fenwick

Research Scientist · Data & sensors

Keeps the Sentinel array running and the data portal honest. Built the quality-control pipeline that flags drift before it reaches a published figure.

RM

Dr. Renée Maillet

Postdoctoral Researcher

Uses environmental DNA as an early-warning system for range-expanding southern fish arriving in the Gulf of Maine.

Where our graduates go

Calderwood-trained scientists now work in state and federal fisheries agencies, conservation nonprofits, consultancies, aquaculture, and faculty positions across North America and Europe. Many remain collaborators, and several co-author the papers in our current bibliography.
