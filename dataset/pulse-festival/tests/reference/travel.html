GETTING THERE

Gullrock Bay sits on the Marran Coast, a two-mile sweep of beach between the harbour and the lighthouse. It's gloriously remote — which is half the magic and the one thing to plan around. Here's every way to reach us, and everywhere to lay your head.

The site

Gullrock Bay,
Marran Coast

The festival wraps around the bay: main arena on the beach, camping rising into the dunes, late-night warehouses along the old harbour, and the Salt Chapel beside the lighthouse path. The nearest town, Marran-on-Sea, is a 15-minute shuttle ride away.

Every route in

How to arrive

We're a low-car festival — the bay roads are narrow and we'd rather they stayed quiet. Two thirds of you came car-free last year. Here's how.

Best option

By Train + Shuttle

Direct services run to Marran Parkway from the city in 1h 50m. Our free, accessible shuttle buses loop from the station forecourt to the main gate every 15 minutes from Thursday noon to Monday midday.

Free shuttle 15-min loop

Direct & easy

By Festival Coach

Carbon-offset return coaches run from 14 cities straight to the camp gate. Reserved seats, step-free options and a luggage hold for your tent. Book a seat with your ticket from £29 return.

From £29 14 cities

If you must

By Car

Pre-book a £25 parking pass — there is no pay-on-the-day parking and the lanes are tow-away zones. Car parks are an 8-minute walk from camping. Car-share spaces near the front for 3+ passengers.

Pass required Car-share perks

Two wheels

By Bike

The Marran Coast Path connects to the National Cycle Network. Free, staffed bike parking sits beside the main gate, plus a repair stand and pump. Arrive smug, leave smugger.

Free parking Repair stand

Scenic route

By Ferry

A seasonal passenger ferry crosses from Port Halden to Marran harbour twice daily — a 40-minute trip with the best arrival view going. Foot passengers only; connects to the shuttle.

2x daily Foot only

On foot

By Coast Path

Already local? The lighthouse path links Marran-on-Sea to the site in a 45-minute clifftop walk. Lit and stewarded after dark each night of the festival. Bring proper shoes.

45-min walk Stewarded

Where to sleep

Stay the weekend

Camping is part of the RIPTIDE ritual, but it's not the only way. Day-trippers and comfort-seekers, we've got you too.

From £40 / weekend

Dune Camping

Bring-your-own-tent pitching in the marram grass, steps from the arena. Hot showers, drinking water, phone charging and 24-hour welfare. Quiet camping zone available.

From £140 / weekend

Bell-Tent Glamping

Pre-pitched, furnished bell tents with real beds, rugs and lanterns. Sleeps two to four. Private showers and a dedicated glamping concierge. Roll in, switch off.

From £80 / night

Marran-on-Sea Stays

B&Bs, the Harbour Inn and seaside guesthouses a short shuttle from site. Limited and very popular — book early. We list official partners with festival rates online.

From £55 / weekend

Campervan Field

Marked bays for vans and small motorhomes with optional electric hook-up. Includes a weekend parking permit. Strictly no overnight sleeping in the day car parks.

Free

Quiet & Family Camp

A calmer corner away from the late-night stages — no sound systems, lights out by midnight. Open to families and anyone who values their sleep. Free to opt into when you book.

No camping

Day Visitors

Day-ticket holders can come and go until 23:00 via the shuttle and coast path. The last shuttle back to the Parkway leaves at 23:45 — set an alarm, the walk is long.

Last questions

Before you set off

What time can I arrive?

Campers can enter from 4pm Thursday. The main arena opens at 11am Friday. Beat the rush and pitch up Thursday evening — there's a welcome bar and a warm-up DJ to ease you in.

Can I bring my own food and drink?

Yes, into the campsite — bring what you like, but no glass anywhere on site. Alcohol can be carried into camp but not into the arena, where our bars take over. Empties go in the right bins, please.

Is the site accessible by wheelchair?

The arena, main paths and one camping field are step-free via boardwalks, with viewing platforms at the High Tide and Sandstorm stages. The dunes and coast path are uneven — plan around them and ask our access team for the best routes.

What's the weather like in August?

Coastal and changeable. Expect warm days, cool nights and the odd sideways sea breeze. Pack layers, a waterproof, sun cream and sturdy footwear — and a swimsuit for the dawn dips.
