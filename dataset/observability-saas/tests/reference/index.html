What you get

A tracing backend that thinks like an SRE.

Every feature is built around the three questions you ask at 3am: what's slow, why, and what is it costing us to know.

Tail-based sampling

Decide what to keep after the trace completes. Persist every error and slow outlier, sample the boring 200s. Set rules per route, per tenant, per cost budget.

Trace explorer

Filter 2.4T spans by service, attribute, status, or latency band and get a waterfall in under a second. Pivot from a flame graph straight to the offending SQL or RPC.

Live service map

An always-current topology built from real span edges — not a config file. See error rate and p99 on every dependency and spot the service quietly degrading.

Cost guardrails

Per-service ingest budgets with hard and soft ceilings. Spanforge tells you the marginal value of each gigabyte before you pay for it, and alerts before overage.

Exemplar-linked metrics

Click any spike on a latency histogram and land in the exact trace that produced it. Metrics, traces, and logs stitched by trace ID — no context switching.

The economics of tracing

Keep the 0.1% of traces worth keeping.

Head-based sampling throws away the slow requests before you know they're slow. Spanforge buffers the full trace, runs your policy when it completes, and retains exactly what's useful.

  • ✓Always keep any trace with an error, a span over your latency SLO, or a flagged customer.
  • ✓Probabilistic baseline for healthy traffic — 1 in 200 by default, tunable live.
  • ✓Budget-aware rates that tighten automatically as you approach an ingest ceiling.
sampling-policy.yaml Policy 
policies:
  - name: keep-errors
    type: status_code
    match: ERROR
    sample_rate: 1.0

  - name: slow-checkout
    type: latency
    route: "/v2/checkout"
    threshold_ms: 250
    sample_rate: 1.0

  - name: baseline
    type: probabilistic
    sample_rate: 0.005   # 1 in 200
