The studio

We make less,
and make it last.

PLINTH is a two-person design studio with a single workshop. We release a piece only when we can't remove anything more from it — and only when we know how to repair it for the next thirty years.

How it started

A chair, then a rule

Ada Sørensen had spent ten years designing furniture that shipped flat and broke quietly. Marco Ferri ran a small joinery near Milan, repairing the chairs other people had given up on. They met over one of those chairs in 2015 and agreed on a frustration before they agreed on anything else.

A year later they made one lounge chair — the piece that became Fold 01 — with a single rule attached: every part had to come apart with a tool a normal person owns. PLINTH grew from that rule, not from a business plan.

What we believe

Restraint is the design

We think most rooms are overfull and most catalogues are too. So we keep ours to seven pieces and revise them slowly. A new product has to be better than adding nothing — a high bar, and the right one.

Monochrome, square-section, soft-edged: the look isn't a style we apply, it's what's left when we stop adding. We'd rather a piece disappear into a life than announce itself in a photograph.

Working principles

Four things we hold to

01

Subtract first

Every decision starts by removing. If a piece works without a part, the part is wrong. We add back only the one thing the object needed.

02

One workshop

We make everything in one place, with people we know by name, so quality is a conversation rather than a clause in a contract.

03

Design for repair

An object you can fix is an object you keep. We design joints to open, finishes to renew, and parts to be replaced one at a time.

04

No false urgency

No seasonal drops, no limited colours, no reasons to buy again next year. The collection changes when it should, not when the calendar says.
