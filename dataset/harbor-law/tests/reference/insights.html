Featured · M&A

Earnouts that survive the relationship.

More deals close on an earnout than ever before — and more earnouts end in dispute. The difference is almost always in the drafting.

An earnout bridges a gap in price by deferring part of it to future performance. In theory it aligns buyer and seller. In practice it binds two parties with opposing incentives to a single set of numbers, often for years. We walk through the four provisions that decide whether an earnout becomes a clean payout or a courtroom — the metric, the control covenants, the acceleration triggers, and the dispute mechanism.
