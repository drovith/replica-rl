A firm built around the partner, not the pyramid.

We started Halloran Voss to practice law the way we believed it should be practiced — senior lawyers, a narrow docket, and counsel measured by judgment rather than volume.

Our Story

Founded in 2009 on a contrarian premise.

Margaret Halloran and Daniel Voss left the partnership of a global firm with a shared frustration: that the clients who most needed senior judgment — founders, general counsel, and mid-market boards — too often received the least of it. The economics of the large firm pushed the real work down the pyramid.

They built Halloran Voss to invert that model. We staff leanly and bill honestly. Partners do the drafting, take the calls, and sit in the negotiations. We turn down matters we cannot serve with the attention they deserve, and we have never regretted it.

Fifteen years on, we remain deliberately small — a single bench of senior corporate lawyers across New York and San Francisco, advising on the transactions and disputes that decide our clients' futures.

What We Stand For

Four principles, non-negotiable.

Judgment over volume

We are paid for the call we make, not the hours we log. Our advice ends in a recommendation, informed by decades of seeing how these deals actually turn out.

Candor, always

If a deal is wrong, or a position will not hold, you will hear it from us first — in plain language, early enough to act on it.

Discretion by default

Our best work is invisible. We protect our clients' confidences as zealously as their interests, and we do not trade on their names.

Continuity of counsel

The partner who wins your trust is the partner who stays on the matter. We do not hand you off, and we do not staff for our convenience.

Who We Serve

The people on the other side of the table from their biggest decision.

Our clients are rarely shopping for the largest firm. They are looking for the lawyer who will treat their matter as if it were the only one.

  • Founders & Management Teams — raising capital, selling, or defending what they have built.
  • General Counsel — who need a senior partner as an extension of a lean in-house team.
  • Boards & Special Committees — navigating combinations, governance, and conflicts.
  • Venture & Growth Investors — structuring rounds, funds, and exits.

Common Questions

How a boutique fits your matter.

Are you large enough to handle a complex deal?

Yes. Our partners spent their formative years at global firms and bring that depth, with co-counsel and specialist networks we deploy when scale is genuinely required. What we will not do is staff a deal with juniors simply to fill a room.

How do you bill?

Transparently. We work on negotiated fixed fees, capped arrangements, or hourly rates depending on the matter, and we tell you which structure we recommend and why. There are no surprises on the invoice.

Will I work with a partner?

Always. A named partner leads every engagement, drafts the consequential documents, and remains your point of contact from first call to closing.

Can you work alongside our existing counsel?

Frequently. We are often brought in as specialist or transaction counsel beside an in-house team or a generalist firm, and we are comfortable defining a clear, collaborative scope.
